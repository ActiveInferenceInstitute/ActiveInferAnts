# Evaluation of PhD Dissertation for NSF 23-615 Grant Relevance

## 1. Introduction

The dissertation titled "Transposing Chemical Reactions to Ecosystems" explores the intersection of chemical reactions and ecosystem dynamics through a novel framework termed the "Shifted Domain." The primary research questions focus on modeling ecosystems as systems of reactions, the role of keystone species as catalysts, and the application of chemical equilibrium principles to biodiversity. Overall, this dissertation presents a significant and innovative approach to understanding ecological systems, which aligns well with the objectives of the NSF 23-615 grant, particularly in advancing knowledge in the field of physics as it relates to interdisciplinary research.

The NSF's mission is to support diverse and innovative research in physics that enhances knowledge and societal benefits. The dissertation's interdisciplinary approach, bridging chemistry and ecology, aligns with the NSF's emphasis on collaboration and broadening participation in STEM fields. The work is relevant, as it introduces a fresh perspective on ecological dynamics that can inform future research and conservation strategies. However, it is essential to note that while the dissertation contributes to ecological understanding, its direct relevance to the core focus areas of physics as outlined in the grant may be limited.

## 5. Methodology and Data Analysis

The dissertation employs a mixed-methods approach, combining qualitative case studies with quantitative modeling. This methodology is appropriate for exploring the proposed hypotheses, as it allows for a comprehensive analysis of ecosystem dynamics.

### Rigor and Effectiveness

The use of statistical modeling and simulation software to analyze species interactions is commendable. The author states, "Statistical modeling will be employed to assess the relationships between species interactions and ecosystem health," indicating a rigorous approach to data analysis. However, the dissertation could benefit from clearer descriptions of the data collection methods and specific analytical techniques used.

### Limitations

- **Empirical Validation**: The reliance on theoretical constructs without sufficient empirical validation may limit the findings' applicability. The author acknowledges, "Long-term studies will be conducted to monitor changes in biodiversity and ecosystem stability," which is essential for robust conclusions.
