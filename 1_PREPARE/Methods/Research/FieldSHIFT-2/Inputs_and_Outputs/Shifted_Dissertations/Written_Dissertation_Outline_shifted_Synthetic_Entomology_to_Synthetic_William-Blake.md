# PhD Dissertation: Transposing Entomology into the Context of William Blake's Life and Work

## Executive Summary
This dissertation aims to explore the innovative intersection between entomology—the study of insects—and the life and works of William Blake, a seminal figure in literature and art. By examining the thematic parallels between insect life cycles and Blake's explorations of transformation, social structures, and interconnectedness, this research seeks to create a new interdisciplinary framework that enriches both fields. The findings will not only enhance the understanding of Blake’s oeuvre but also provide insights into ecological themes, potentially influencing contemporary environmental discourse.

## Introduction

### Background of the Shifted Domain
The fusion of entomology and Blake studies represents an unprecedented approach to understanding both the natural and cultural worlds. Entomology offers a rich tapestry of biological and ecological insights, including the complex behaviors, life cycles, and social structures of insects. Blake’s works, characterized by their profound commentary on human existence, societal structures, and spiritual transformations, provide fertile ground for an interdisciplinary analysis. This dissertation will examine how these domains intersect, revealing deeper meanings and novel interpretations that challenge traditional literary criticism and ecological studies.

### Significance and Novelty of the Research
This research is significant in its potential to transform literary criticism and ecological studies. By applying entomological concepts to Blake's themes, the dissertation will highlight the relevance of ecological perspectives in literary analysis. The interdisciplinary framework proposed herein will foster a deeper appreciation for both disciplines, offering new ways to interpret Blake’s work and to understand the ecological implications of literature. This novel approach not only enriches Blake studies but also contributes to the discourse on environmental consciousness in literature.

### Overarching Research Questions and Objectives
1. How can the life cycles and behaviors of insects inform our understanding of transformation in Blake's poetry and art?
2. In what ways do the social structures of insect societies reflect or critique human societal norms in Blake’s work?
3. What new theoretical frameworks can be developed from the integration of entomology and Blake studies?

## Literature Review

### Historical Context of the Original Domains
The development of entomology as a scientific discipline has roots in ancient civilizations, with significant advancements occurring during the Renaissance and the Enlightenment. Pioneers such as Carl Linnaeus established systematic classifications of insects, laying the groundwork for modern entomological studies. Concurrently, the life of William Blake (1757-1827) unfolded against a backdrop of social upheaval, industrialization, and burgeoning Romanticism. His artistic influences, ranging from Milton to the burgeoning scientific discourse of his time, shaped his unique vision of the world.

### Current State of Knowledge in Both Fields
Contemporary research in entomology has expanded to include ecological roles, behaviors, and the symbolic meanings attributed to insects in various cultures. Scholars have explored the intricate relationships between insects and their environments, emphasizing their essential roles in ecosystems. In parallel, current interpretations of Blake’s works have delved into themes of duality, transformation, and social critique, often focusing on the dichotomy between innocence and experience. However, a significant gap exists in the interdisciplinary dialogue between these two fields.

### Gaps and Opportunities Presented by the Shifted Domain
The lack of interdisciplinary research combining entomology with literary studies presents a unique opportunity for exploration. By identifying parallels between insect life and Blake’s thematic concerns, this dissertation aims to bridge the divide between the sciences and the humanities, offering fresh insights into both entomological phenomena and Blake’s literary contributions.

## Theoretical Framework

### Foundational Theories from Original Domains
Ecological theories related to insect behavior and adaptation, such as the concept of niche construction and the dynamics of social insects, provide a basis for understanding the complex interactions within ecosystems. In literary studies, theories relevant to Blake’s work, including Romanticism, symbolism, and psychoanalysis, offer frameworks for analyzing his themes of transformation and societal critique.

### New Theoretical Constructs Emerging from the Shift
This dissertation proposes the concept of "Insectual Metamorphosis" as a metaphor for spiritual transformation, drawing parallels between the physical transformations of insects and the psychological and spiritual journeys depicted in Blake’s poetry. Additionally, "Societal Entomology" emerges as a framework for analyzing human social dynamics through the lens of insect behavior, allowing for a critique of conformity and individuality within both realms.

### Proposed Integrated Theoretical Model
The development of an interdisciplinary model that combines ecological insights with literary analysis will facilitate a holistic understanding of Blake's themes. This model will enable the exploration of how entomological concepts can inform interpretations of human experience, ultimately enriching both entomology and literary criticism.

## Methodology

### Research Design Overview
The research design will employ a qualitative approach, utilizing comparative analysis and thematic exploration as primary methods. This design will enable a nuanced understanding of the intersections between entomology and Blake’s work.

### Data Collection Methods
Data collection will consist of two primary components: textual analysis of Blake’s poetry and visual art, and field studies observing insect behaviors and life cycles. The textual analysis will focus on identifying themes of transformation and social structures, while field studies will provide empirical insights into insect life.

### Analytical Approaches
Thematic coding of Blake’s works will identify parallels with entomological concepts, allowing for a comparative study linking insect social structures with human societal themes in Blake’s writings. This approach will facilitate a deeper understanding of how Blake’s artistic vision resonates with ecological realities.

### Ethical Considerations
Ethical considerations will be paramount in this research, particularly in ensuring the humane treatment of living organisms during field studies. Additionally, addressing potential biases in literary interpretation will be crucial to maintaining academic integrity.

## Core Chapters

### Key Aspect 1: Metamorphosis as a Literary Device

#### Sub-section 1: Reimagining Characters
In this section, the dissertation will analyze Blake's characters through the lens of insect metamorphosis. Characters such as the child in "Songs of Innocence" and the adult in "Songs of Experience" can be reimagined as representations of the transformative life cycles of insects, mirroring the processes of growth and change inherent in both nature and human experience.

#### Sub-section 2: Spiritual and Psychological Transformations
This subsection will explore how transformations in nature reflect human spiritual journeys in Blake’s work. The cyclical nature of insect life can serve as a powerful metaphor for the spiritual rebirth and psychological evolution experienced by Blake's protagonists, emphasizing themes of renewal and transcendence.

### Key Aspect 2: Social Structures and Insect Societies

#### Sub-section 1: Social Commentary
This section will investigate parallels between insect hierarchies and human societal structures in Blake’s critique. The intricate social dynamics of ant colonies or bee hives can illuminate Blake’s commentary on the rigidity of human social norms, offering a fresh perspective on his critiques of industrial society.

#### Sub-section 2: Individuality vs. Conformity
Examining themes of individuality in Blake’s work, this subsection will draw connections to insect behavior, particularly in species known for their social structures. The tension between individuality and conformity in Blake’s poetry can be mirrored in the behaviors of social insects, highlighting the complexities of identity within collective societies.

### Key Aspect 3: Symbolism of Insects

#### Sub-section 1: Insects as Symbols
This section will discuss the symbolic meanings of various insects in Blake’s art and poetry. Insects such as the butterfly, often associated with transformation, or the spider, symbolizing creativity and entrapment, will be analyzed for their deeper implications within Blake’s thematic framework.

#### Sub-section 2: Ecological and Spiritual Symbolism
Analyzing how insect symbolism reflects ecological themes and spiritual beliefs, this subsection will explore the duality of insects as both agents of destruction and symbols of renewal, paralleling the complexities of human existence in Blake’s work.

### Key Aspect 4: Generating Novel Hypotheses and Theories

#### Sub-section 1: Insect Metamorphosis as a Metaphor for Human Transformation
This section will present a hypothesis and experimental design for analyzing themes of growth and transformation in Blake’s poetry through the lens of insect metamorphosis. This research could involve comparative literary analysis and ecological studies to validate the metaphorical connections.

#### Sub-section 2: Social Insect Behavior as a Reflection of Human Society
Discussing a hypothesis and comparative study design on social dynamics, this subsection will investigate how the behaviors of social insects can inform our understanding of human societal structures, particularly in relation to themes of cooperation, competition, and social hierarchy in Blake’s work.
