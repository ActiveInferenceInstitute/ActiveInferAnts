# Literature Review

## Historical Context of the Original Domains

### Mitochondrial Biology

Mitochondria are double-membrane-bound organelles found in the cytoplasm of nearly all eukaryotic cells. They are primarily known for their role in adenosine triphosphate (ATP) production through oxidative phosphorylation, a process that converts biochemical energy from nutrients into ATP, which cells use for various functions. Historical discoveries in cellular respiration and metabolism, beginning in the mid-nineteenth century with the work of scientists like Richard Altmann and later Hans Adolf Krebs, have paved the way for our current understanding of mitochondrial function. These discoveries have shown that mitochondria are not only energy producers but also play critical roles in regulating cellular metabolism, apoptosis, and signaling pathways.

### William Blake's Life and Work

William Blake (1757-1827) was an English poet, painter, and printmaker whose work has had a lasting impact on literature and art. His unique blending of the visual and verbal, along with his profound philosophical and spiritual inquiries, set him apart as a seminal figure in the Romantic movement. Blake's life was marked by personal and societal upheaval, and his works often reflect a deep engagement with themes of innocence, experience, and the critique of industrialization and social injustice. By examining the societal influences on Blake's work, we can better understand the context of his artistic output and the motivations behind his critiques.

## Current State of Knowledge in Both Fields

### Mitochondrial Research

Recent advancements in mitochondrial genetics have revealed the complexities of mitochondrial DNA (mtDNA), its inheritance patterns, and its implications for health and disease. The field has expanded to include studies on mitochondrial dysfunction, which is now recognized as a contributing factor to a variety of diseases, including neurodegenerative disorders, metabolic syndromes, and cardiovascular diseases. Current theories emphasize the connection between mitochondrial health and overall well-being, suggesting that the functioning of these organelles is integral to maintaining cellular and systemic health.

### Blake Studies

Contemporary interpretations of Blake's art and poetry have evolved, with scholars increasingly recognizing the multifaceted nature of his work. Ongoing debates regarding Blake's influence and legacy focus on his innovative techniques, thematic depth, and the socio-political context of his creations. Recent scholarship has also explored the spiritual dimensions of Blake's work, offering new insights into his understanding of the human experience and the role of the artist in society.

## Gaps and Opportunities Presented by the Shifted Domain

Despite the rich scholarship in both mitochondrial biology and Blake studies, there exists a notable gap in interdisciplinary approaches that integrate biological concepts with literary analysis. This research presents an opportunity for innovative frameworks that enhance understanding of creativity and societal health. By drawing parallels between mitochondrial function and Blake's artistic expression, this dissertation aims to fill this gap, offering a fresh perspective that enriches both fields.

### Blakean Theory

Blakean theory encompasses the duality of innocence and experience, a central theme in Blake's work that reflects the tension between the ideal and the real. This duality is further complicated by Blake's critique of industrialization and social injustice, which serves as a backdrop for his exploration of the human condition. ## New Theoretical Constructs Emerging from the Shift

### Creative Biogenesis Theory

The Creative Biogenesis Theory posits that artistic evolution is akin to a biological process of revitalization. Just as mitochondrial biogenesis involves the growth and division of mitochondria to adapt to cellular demands, artistic expression evolves in response to societal and personal stimuli. This theory suggests that creativity is a dynamic process influenced by both internal and external factors.

### Oxidative Imagination

The Oxidative Imagination concept proposes that creativity embodies a dual nature—serving as both a source of inspiration and a potential for chaos. This notion draws parallels between the oxidative processes in mitochondria, which can lead to both energy production and oxidative stress, and the creative process, which can yield profound insights while also posing risks of emotional and psychological turmoil.

The proposed integrated theoretical model links mitochondrial functions to artistic vitality, metabolic adaptability, societal critique, and cultural influence.
