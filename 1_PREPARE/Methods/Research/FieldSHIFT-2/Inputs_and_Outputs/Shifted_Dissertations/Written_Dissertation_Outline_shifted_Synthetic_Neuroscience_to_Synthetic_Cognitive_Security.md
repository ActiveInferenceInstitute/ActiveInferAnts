# PhD Dissertation: Transposing Neuroscience into the Context of Cognitive Security

This dissertation explores the innovative fusion of neuroscience and cognitive security, presenting a comprehensive framework that leverages insights from neural processes to enhance cognitive resilience against misinformation and cognitive threats. The potential to revolutionize educational paradigms, inform policy-making, and foster interdisciplinary collaboration underscores the transformative capacity of this Shifted Domain.

## Introduction

### Background of the Shifted Domain

The intersection of neuroscience and cognitive security represents a groundbreaking shift in understanding how cognitive processes can be safeguarded against manipulation and deception. Neuroscience, the scientific study of the nervous system, provides critical insights into the brain's architecture and functioning. It examines how neural connections and processes underpin cognitive functions such as perception, memory, and decision-making. Conversely, cognitive security focuses on protecting cognitive processes from threats like misinformation, cognitive biases, and social engineering, which can undermine individual and collective decision-making capabilities.

This dissertation aims to explore the synergies between these domains. By transposing principles from neuroscience onto cognitive security, we can develop a novel framework for analyzing and mitigating cognitive threats. The interplay between neural mechanisms and cognitive vulnerabilities opens new avenues for understanding and addressing the challenges posed by misinformation and cognitive manipulation.

The significance of this research lies in its potential to enhance cognitive security measures by applying principles derived from neuroscience. The novelty of this research is rooted in its ability to transpose neural principles onto cognitive security, creating a robust theoretical foundation for future exploration.

This dissertation aims to make a substantial contribution to both neuroscience and cognitive security by providing a systematic analysis of how neural mechanisms can inform strategies for enhancing cognitive resilience.
