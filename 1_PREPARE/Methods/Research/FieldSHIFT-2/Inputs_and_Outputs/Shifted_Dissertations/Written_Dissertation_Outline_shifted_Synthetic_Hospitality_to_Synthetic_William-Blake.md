# PhD Dissertation: Transposing Guest Experience in Hospitality to William Blake's Life and Work

## Executive Summary

This dissertation explores the innovative intersection of guest experience principles in hospitality and the life and work of William Blake. By analyzing the holistic and personalized nature of guest interactions and applying these frameworks to Blake's artistic and literary contributions, this research aims to create a new understanding of audience engagement in both fields. The significance of this study lies in its potential to redefine how we interpret and experience art and literature, fostering deeper connections and enriching the visitor experience in cultural institutions. This work will contribute to both hospitality studies and literary criticism, paving the way for interdisciplinary collaborations and applications.

## Literature Review

### Historical Context of the Original Domains

#### Guest Experience in Hospitality

The evolution of guest experience principles in hospitality can be traced back to the early 20th century, where the focus was primarily on service quality and customer satisfaction. Pioneering works by scholars such as Parasuraman et al. (1985) introduced the SERVQUAL model, which laid the foundation for understanding service quality dimensions. Over time, the concept of guest experience has evolved to encompass holistic interactions that prioritize personalization, emotional engagement, and the creation of memorable experiences. 

Recent advancements in technology have further transformed guest experience, enabling the integration of data analytics to tailor services to individual preferences. The rise of the digital age has prompted hospitality professionals to adopt innovative strategies that enhance guest interactions, thereby fostering loyalty and satisfaction.

#### William Blake's Life and Work

William Blake (1757-1827) was a pivotal figure in the Romantic movement, known for his visionary art and poetry that challenged societal norms and explored the depths of human experience. His works, including "Songs of Innocence and of Experience" and "The Marriage of Heaven and Hell," reflect a profound engagement with themes of duality, imagination, and the human condition. Blake's unique artistic techniques, characterized by the interplay of text and image, invite audiences to engage with his work on multiple levels, fostering a rich interpretive landscape.

Understanding Blake's historical context is essential for appreciating the nuances of his artistic contributions. The socio-political climate of late 18th-century Britain, marked by industrialization and social upheaval, profoundly influenced Blake's critique of contemporary society. His works serve as a lens through which we can explore the complexities of human experience, making them particularly relevant in the context of audience engagement.

### Current State of Knowledge in Both Fields

#### Guest Experience

Contemporary research in hospitality emphasizes the importance of personalization and technology integration in enhancing guest experiences. Scholars such as Pine and Gilmore (1999) have articulated the concept of the "experience economy," where businesses strive to create memorable experiences that resonate with guests on an emotional level. Recent studies have explored the impact of digital tools, such as mobile applications and virtual reality, on guest engagement, highlighting their potential to enrich interactions and foster loyalty.

#### Blake Studies

Current scholarship on Blake has focused on various interpretative frameworks, including psychoanalytic, feminist, and post-colonial readings. Scholars such as Northrop Frye and Harold Bloom have contributed significantly to the understanding of Blake's literary techniques and thematic concerns. However, there remains a gap in the literature regarding interdisciplinary approaches that bridge hospitality and literary studies, particularly in the context of audience engagement.

## Theoretical Framework

#### Service Quality Models

The study of service quality in hospitality is grounded in several foundational theories that elucidate the dimensions of guest satisfaction. The SERVQUAL model, for instance, identifies five key dimensions: tangibles, reliability, responsiveness, assurance, and empathy. These dimensions serve as a framework for assessing service quality and can be adapted to evaluate audience engagement with Blake's work.

Romanticism, as a literary movement, emphasizes the importance of individual experience, emotion, and imagination.
