# PhD Dissertation: Transposing Mitochondrial Biology to Guest Experience in Hospitality

## Executive Summary

This dissertation aims to explore the innovative intersection of mitochondrial biology and guest experience in the hospitality industry. By drawing parallels between mitochondrial functions and guest interactions, the research seeks to develop new theoretical constructs and practical applications that enhance guest satisfaction and operational efficiency. The proposed framework—integrating concepts such as "experience energy," dynamic service models, and experience biogenesis—has the potential to revolutionize hospitality management, foster guest loyalty, and create sustainable business practices. This work invites interdisciplinary collaboration and contributes to the academic discourse on experiential service delivery.

## Introduction

### Background of the Shifted Domain

The fusion of mitochondrial biology and hospitality guest experience represents a novel approach to understanding and improving service delivery. Mitochondria, known as the powerhouses of the cell, are crucial for energy production and cellular health. They perform essential functions, including ATP (adenosine triphosphate) synthesis, regulation of metabolic pathways, and apoptosis (programmed cell death). Similarly, guest experience serves as the lifeblood of the hospitality industry, driving satisfaction and loyalty. In a competitive market, the quality of guest interactions significantly influences brand perception and repeat business, akin to how mitochondrial efficiency impacts overall cellular function.

### Significance and Novelty of the Research

This research is significant as it introduces biological principles to hospitality management, offering fresh insights into guest engagement and operational strategies. The novelty lies in the transposition of biological concepts into a service-oriented framework, which has not been previously explored. By employing mitochondrial dynamics as a metaphor for service interactions, this dissertation aims to bridge the gap between two seemingly disparate fields, providing a foundation for innovative hospitality practices.

### Overarching Research Questions and Objectives

1. How can mitochondrial principles be applied to enhance guest experience in hospitality?
2. What is the relationship between "experience energy" and guest loyalty?
3. In what ways can dynamic service models improve operational efficiency and guest satisfaction?

## Literature Review

### Historical Context of the Original Domains

#### Mitochondrial Biology

Mitochondria were first identified in the mid-19th century, and their role in cellular respiration was elucidated in the early 20th century. Understanding mitochondrial function has evolved significantly, with current research focusing on their role in energy metabolism, signaling pathways, and apoptosis. Mitochondrial dysfunction is linked to various diseases, including neurodegenerative disorders and metabolic syndromes, highlighting their importance in cellular health.

#### Guest Experience in Hospitality

The concept of guest experience has evolved from basic service provision to a comprehensive understanding of the emotional and psychological interactions between guests and service providers. Historically, hospitality focused on tangible aspects such as food and accommodation. However, the late 20th century saw a shift towards experiential marketing, emphasizing emotional connections and personalized services. This evolution has led to the development of various models for measuring guest satisfaction and loyalty.

### Current State of Knowledge in Both Fields

#### Mitochondrial Research

Recent advancements in mitochondrial research have revealed their complex roles beyond energy production, including involvement in cell signaling, inflammation, and aging. Studies have shown that mitochondrial health is crucial for maintaining overall physiological well-being, suggesting potential applications for improving human interactions through an understanding of energy dynamics.

#### Guest Experience Management

Current trends in guest experience management include the integration of technology, personalization of services, and a focus on emotional engagement. The rise of digital platforms has transformed how guests interact with hospitality services, allowing for real-time feedback and tailored experiences. However, there remains a lack of comprehensive models that integrate these advancements with a biological understanding of energy dynamics.

### Gaps and Opportunities Presented by the Shifted Domain

There is a notable absence of interdisciplinary studies bridging biology and hospitality. This gap presents an opportunity to innovate service delivery models by applying biological principles. By examining the parallels between mitochondrial functions and guest interactions, this research aims to fill this void and propose a new framework for enhancing guest satisfaction.

## Theoretical Framework

### Foundational Theories from Original Domains

#### Energy Production in Mitochondria

Mitochondria generate ATP through oxidative phosphorylation, a process that involves the electron transport chain and chemiosmosis. This energy production is vital for cellular functions, and disruptions in this process can lead to cellular dysfunction and disease. Understanding these dynamics can provide insights into how energy levels affect human interactions and experiences.

#### Theories of Service Quality and Guest Satisfaction

The SERVQUAL model, developed by Parasuraman, Zeithaml, and Berry, identifies five dimensions of service quality: tangibles, reliability, responsiveness, assurance, and empathy. These dimensions play a crucial role in shaping guest satisfaction and loyalty. Integrating these theories with biological concepts can lead to a more holistic understanding of guest experience.

### New Theoretical Constructs Emerging from the Shift

#### Experience Energy Theory

Experience Energy Theory posits that the cumulative effect of positive guest interactions generates a form of "experience energy" that enhances overall satisfaction and loyalty. This theory draws parallels between the energy produced by mitochondria and the emotional energy generated through positive service encounters.

#### Dynamic Service Adaptation Model

The Dynamic Service Adaptation Model proposes that service delivery should be agile, adapting to the changing needs and preferences of guests in real-time. This model draws on the concepts of mitochondrial fusion and fission, where mitochondria dynamically adjust their structure and function based on cellular demands.

### Proposed Integrated Theoretical Model

An integrated model will be developed that combines mitochondrial dynamics with guest experience frameworks. This model will illustrate the interactions between experience energy, service adaptation, and guest satisfaction, providing a comprehensive understanding of how biological principles can inform hospitality practices.
