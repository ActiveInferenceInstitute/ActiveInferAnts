# PhD Dissertation Plan: Transforming Guest Experience through the Spatial Web

## Executive Summary
This dissertation proposes to explore the transformative potential of integrating the Spatial Web with the guest experience in hospitality. By leveraging technologies such as Augmented Reality (AR), Internet of Things (IoT), and edge computing, this research aims to redefine how guests interact with their environments, ultimately enhancing personalization, operational efficiency, and overall satisfaction. The significance of this research lies in its interdisciplinary approach, bridging technology and hospitality to create a new paradigm in guest experiences. The potential impact extends beyond hospitality, influencing other sectors and contributing to the development of smart, sustainable environments.

## Introduction
### Background of the Shifted Domain
The fusion of the Spatial Web and guest experience represents a significant evolution in how physical and digital realms interact, particularly in hospitality. This integration allows for seamless user engagement, data utilization, and real-time processing, creating immersive and personalized experiences.

### Significance and Novelty of the Research
This research is novel as it combines two previously distinct domains, offering a fresh perspective on enhancing guest experiences through advanced technologies. It addresses current challenges in hospitality, such as personalization and service quality, in an era of rapid technological advancement.

### Overarching Research Questions and Objectives
1. How can technologies from the Spatial Web enhance the guest experience in hospitality?
2. What are the implications of integrating IoT and AR for operational efficiency and guest satisfaction?
3. What new theoretical constructs emerge from this integration, and how can they be operationalized?

## Core Chapters
### Key Aspect 1: Augmented Reality Applications in Hospitality
#### Sub-section 1: AR for Check-in Processes
- Hypothesis: Implementing AR in check-in can reduce wait times and enhance guest satisfaction.
- Proposed Experiment: A/B testing of AR check-in versus traditional methods.

#### Sub-section 2: AR for Interactive Hotel Tours
- Hypothesis: AR can improve guest engagement and satisfaction by providing immersive experiences.
- Proposed Experiment: Measuring guest satisfaction before and after AR implementation.

### Key Aspect 2: Smart Hospitality Solutions
#### Sub-section 1: IoT-Enabled Smart Rooms
- Hypothesis: IoT devices can significantly increase guest satisfaction through personalized environments.
- Proposed Experiment: Comparison of guest feedback in IoT-enabled versus traditional rooms.

#### Sub-section 2: Digital Twins for Real-time Monitoring
- Hypothesis: Digital twins can enhance operational efficiency in hospitality management.
- Proposed Experiment: Case study analysis of hotels using digital twins for maintenance and guest services.

### Key Aspect 3: Data-Driven Personalization
#### Sub-section 1: Geospatial Data Utilization
- Hypothesis: Utilizing geospatial data can lead to improved personalized recommendations for guests.
- Proposed Experiment: Analyzing booking patterns and guest feedback before and after data integration.

#### Sub-section 2: Real-time Feedback Mechanisms
- Hypothesis: Real-time feedback can enhance service recovery strategies and guest satisfaction.
- Proposed Experiment: Implementation of real-time feedback systems and analysis of service recovery outcomes.

### Key Aspect 4: Edge Computing for Real-time Service
#### Sub-section 1: Edge Computing Applications
- Hypothesis: Edge computing can improve response times for guest inquiries and requests.
- Proposed Experiment: Measuring response times and guest satisfaction levels before and after edge computing implementation.

#### Sub-section 2: Impact on Operational Efficiency
- Hypothesis: Edge computing can lead to significant improvements in operational efficiency in hospitality.
- Proposed Experiment: Case study analysis of hotels using edge computing technologies.
