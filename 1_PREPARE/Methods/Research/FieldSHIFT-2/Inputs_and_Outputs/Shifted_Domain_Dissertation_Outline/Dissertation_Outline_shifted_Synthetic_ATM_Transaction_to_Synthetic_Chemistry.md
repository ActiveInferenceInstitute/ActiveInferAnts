# PhD Dissertation Plan: Transposing ATM Transactions to Chemistry

## Executive Summary
This dissertation aims to explore the innovative intersection of ATM transactions and chemistry, proposing a novel framework that leverages principles from banking technology to enhance chemical education, research, and processes. By analyzing the structural similarities between transaction flows and chemical reactions, this research will contribute to the development of user-centered design in chemistry laboratories and real-time data analytics in chemical processes. The potential impact includes improved educational outcomes, enhanced efficiency in chemical reactions, and the establishment of new interdisciplinary fields.

## Introduction
### Background of the Shifted Domain
The integration of ATM transaction principles into chemistry presents a unique opportunity to enhance both fields. ATMs operate on a series of secure, user-friendly protocols designed for efficiency and accessibility, while chemistry relies on complex interactions and reactions that can benefit from similar methodologies.

### Significance and Novelty of the Research
This research is significant as it introduces a transformative approach to understanding chemical processes through the lens of transactional interactions. It challenges traditional paradigms in chemistry and proposes innovative methodologies that prioritize user experience and data-driven decision-making.

### Overarching Research Questions and Objectives
1. How can user-centered design principles improve engagement and learning outcomes in chemistry education?
2. In what ways can real-time data analytics enhance the predictability and efficiency of chemical reactions?
3. What new interdisciplinary frameworks can emerge from the fusion of ATM transaction principles and chemical processes?

## Literature Review
### Historical Context of the Original Domains
- **ATM Transactions:** Evolution from manual banking to automated systems, emphasizing self-service and security.
- **Chemistry:** Development from classical chemistry to modern chemical theories, focusing on molecular interactions and reaction dynamics.

### Current State of Knowledge in Both Fields
- **ATM Transactions:** Advances in contactless payments, mobile banking, and cybersecurity.
- **Chemistry:** Innovations in catalysis, reaction mechanisms, and environmental chemistry.

### Gaps and Opportunities Presented by the Shifted Domain
- Lack of interdisciplinary research linking user experience design with chemical education and processes.
- Opportunities to apply data analytics in chemistry for improved outcomes and efficiency.

## Theoretical Framework
### Foundational Theories from Original Domains
- **User-Centered Design (UCD):** Principles focused on optimizing user interaction and satisfaction.
- **Reaction Mechanisms:** Theories explaining how chemical reactions occur through a series of steps.

### New Theoretical Constructs Emerging from the Shift
- **Chemical Transaction Theory:** A conceptual model that parallels ATM transactions with chemical reactions.
- **Data-Driven Chemistry:** A framework for applying real-time data analytics to chemical processes.

### Proposed Integrated Theoretical Model
An integrated model that combines UCD principles with reaction mechanisms to create a comprehensive understanding of chemical transactions.
