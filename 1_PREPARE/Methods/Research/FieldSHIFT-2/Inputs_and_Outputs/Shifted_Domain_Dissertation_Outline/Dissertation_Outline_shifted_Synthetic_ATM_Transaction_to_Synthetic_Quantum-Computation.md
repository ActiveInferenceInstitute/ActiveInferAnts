# PhD Dissertation Plan: Transposing ATM Transaction Concepts into Quantum Computation

## Executive Summary
This dissertation aims to explore the innovative intersection of Automated Teller Machine (ATM) transaction principles and quantum computation. By analyzing the core concepts of self-service operation, transaction flows, and security protocols from the ATM domain, the research will develop a user-centric framework for quantum computing interfaces. This fusion not only promises to enhance the accessibility and usability of quantum technologies but also addresses existing challenges in quantum algorithm design and security. The potential impact includes improved quantum user experiences, enhanced algorithmic efficiency, and novel security protocols, thereby revolutionizing the landscape of quantum computing and its applications in various industries.

## Introduction

### Background of the Shifted Domain
The ATM domain has evolved significantly, transitioning from manual banking to automated, self-service transactions. Simultaneously, quantum computation has emerged as a revolutionary field, leveraging principles of quantum mechanics to process information. This dissertation seeks to bridge these two domains, creating a novel framework that transposes ATM transaction concepts into quantum computing.

### Significance and Novelty of the Research
This research is significant as it addresses the gap between complex quantum processes and user experience. By applying established ATM principles, the dissertation will contribute to making quantum computing more approachable for non-experts, thereby fostering broader adoption and innovation in the field.

### Overarching Research Questions and Objectives
1. How can user-centric design principles from ATM transactions enhance quantum computing interfaces?
2. What structured transaction flows can be developed for quantum algorithms to improve efficiency?
3. How can ATM security protocols inform the development of quantum cryptographic measures?
