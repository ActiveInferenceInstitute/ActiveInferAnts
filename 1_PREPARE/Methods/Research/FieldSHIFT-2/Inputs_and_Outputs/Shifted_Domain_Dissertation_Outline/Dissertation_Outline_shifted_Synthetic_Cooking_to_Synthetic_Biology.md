# PhD Dissertation Plan: Transposing Cuisine to Biology

## Executive Summary
This dissertation explores the innovative fusion of culinary arts and biological sciences, positing that principles from cuisine can be effectively transposed to enhance our understanding of ecosystems and biodiversity. The significance of this research lies in its potential to create a new interdisciplinary framework that redefines ecological management, sustainability practices, and educational methodologies. By developing a comprehensive theoretical model and empirical investigations, this work aims to contribute significantly to both fields, providing novel insights and practical applications that address contemporary environmental challenges.

---

## Introduction

### Background of the Shifted Domain
The intersection of cuisine and biology represents a groundbreaking domain shift that examines how culinary principles can inform ecological practices. By analyzing the similarities in processes such as flavor profiling and nutrient cycling, this research seeks to bridge the gap between two traditionally distinct fields.

### Significance and Novelty of the Research
This dissertation is significant as it challenges conventional boundaries by integrating culinary methodologies with biological frameworks. The novelty lies in the development of new theoretical constructs and practical applications that enhance ecosystem management and promote sustainability.

### Overarching Research Questions and Objectives
- How can culinary principles be applied to biological frameworks to improve ecosystem health?
- What novel methodologies can be developed to assess biodiversity through the lens of cuisine?
- In what ways can this interdisciplinary approach foster innovative educational practices?
