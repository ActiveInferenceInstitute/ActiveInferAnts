# PhD Dissertation Plan: Transposing Negotiation to Biology and Ecosystems

## Executive Summary
This dissertation aims to explore the innovative intersection between negotiation and biology, particularly within ecosystems. By transposing principles of negotiation onto biological interactions, this research seeks to redefine our understanding of species dynamics, enhance ecosystem management strategies, and foster resilience in the face of environmental challenges. The findings are expected to contribute significantly to both ecological theory and practical applications in conservation, ultimately promoting a more nuanced understanding of interdependence in natural systems.

## Introduction

### Background of the Shifted Domain
The Shifted Domain represents a novel fusion of negotiation strategies and biological principles, offering a new lens through which to view ecosystem interactions. Negotiation, traditionally seen as a human-centric process, shares striking similarities with biological interactions, where organisms negotiate resources, territory, and survival.

### Significance and Novelty of the Research
This research is significant as it bridges two previously distinct fields, fostering interdisciplinary dialogue and innovation. The novelty lies in applying negotiation frameworks to biological systems, offering fresh insights into species interactions and ecosystem dynamics.

### Overarching Research Questions and Objectives
1. How can negotiation principles enhance our understanding of species interactions within ecosystems?
2. What are the implications of viewing ecosystem dynamics through the lens of negotiation?
3. How can these insights inform practical strategies for ecosystem management and conservation?

## Literature Review

### Historical Context of the Original Domains
- **Negotiation**: Evolution from simple bargaining to complex frameworks in diplomacy and business.
- **Biology and Ecosystems**: Development of ecological theories focusing on species interactions, biodiversity, and ecosystem services.

### Current State of Knowledge in Both Fields
- **Negotiation**: Emphasis on collaborative approaches and ethical considerations.
- **Biology**: Ongoing challenges related to biodiversity loss, climate change, and habitat degradation.

### Gaps and Opportunities Presented by the Shifted Domain
- Lack of interdisciplinary research connecting negotiation and ecology.

## Theoretical Framework

### Foundational Theories from Original Domains
- **Negotiation Theory**: Active listening, cultural awareness, flexibility, and creativity.
- **Ecological Theory**: Ecosystem dynamics, biodiversity, and energy flow.

### New Theoretical Constructs Emerging from the Shift
- **Negotiation Ecology**: A framework that integrates negotiation principles into ecological interactions.
- **Mutualistic Negotiation Theory**: Proposes that species engage in negotiation-like behaviors to establish beneficial relationships.

### Proposed Integrated Theoretical Model
An integrated model that combines negotiation strategies with ecological principles, illustrating how negotiation can optimize species interactions and enhance ecosystem resilience.

## Methodology

### Research Design Overview
This research will adopt a mixed-methods approach, combining qualitative and quantitative methods to explore the intersection of negotiation and ecology.

### Data Collection Methods
- **Field Studies**: Observational studies in various ecosystems (e.g., coral reefs, forests).
- **Surveys and Interviews**: Engaging ecologists and negotiation experts to gather insights on species interactions and negotiation strategies.

### Analytical Approaches
- **Statistical Analysis**: To assess correlations between negotiation-like behaviors and ecosystem health.
- **Thematic Analysis**: For qualitative data from interviews and surveys.

### Ethical Considerations
- Ensure ethical treatment of study subjects and adherence to ecological research guidelines.
- Obtain necessary permits for field studies and ensure informed consent from participants.
