# PhD Dissertation Plan: Transforming Guest Experience in Hospitality into the Spatial Web Context

## Executive Summary
This dissertation explores the transformative potential of integrating guest experience principles from the hospitality industry with the emerging Spatial Web context. It aims to create a comprehensive framework for enhancing user engagement through personalized, immersive interactions that bridge physical and digital realms. The research will contribute to both fields by addressing existing gaps, proposing innovative methodologies, and establishing new theoretical constructs. The potential impact includes improved guest satisfaction, enhanced operational efficiencies, and the creation of new sub-disciplines that merge technology with hospitality.

## Introduction

### Background of the Shifted Domain
The hospitality industry has long focused on guest experience as a core tenet of service delivery. Concurrently, the Spatial Web has emerged as a paradigm that integrates physical and digital environments, utilizing technologies such as AR, VR, and IoT. This dissertation seeks to explore the intersection of these domains, examining how principles of guest experience can inform and enhance interactions within the Spatial Web.

### Significance and Novelty of the Research
This research is significant as it addresses the need for innovative approaches to guest engagement in an increasingly digital world. By leveraging the strengths of both domains, the dissertation will introduce new methodologies and theoretical frameworks that can revolutionize how hospitality services are delivered and experienced.

### Overarching Research Questions and Objectives
1. How can principles of guest experience be effectively integrated into the Spatial Web to enhance user engagement?
2. What new theoretical constructs emerge from this integration?
3. How can technology-driven innovations improve guest satisfaction and loyalty in hospitality settings?
