# PhD Dissertation Plan: Transposing the Spatial Web into Neuroscience

## Executive Summary
This dissertation aims to explore the transformative potential of integrating principles from the Spatial Web into the field of Neuroscience. By examining the dynamic interactions between digital environments and neural processes, this research will provide novel insights into neural plasticity, contextual awareness, and immersive learning. The findings are expected to impact both fields significantly, fostering interdisciplinary collaboration and paving the way for innovative educational and therapeutic approaches.

## Introduction
### Background of the Shifted Domain
The Spatial Web represents a convergence of physical and digital realities, allowing for real-time interactions and contextual awareness. Neuroscience, on the other hand, delves into the complexities of the nervous system. This dissertation seeks to bridge these two domains, creating a novel framework for understanding neural processes through the lens of interactive digital technologies.

### Significance and Novelty of the Research
This research is groundbreaking in its approach to merging the Spatial Web's interactive and immersive elements with the study of neural dynamics. By proposing a new integrated framework, this dissertation aims to redefine our understanding of neural processes and learning mechanisms, thereby creating a paradigm shift in both fields.

### Overarching Research Questions and Objectives
- How can principles from the Spatial Web inform our understanding of neural dynamics and plasticity?
- What are the implications of immersive environments on learning and cognitive processes?
- How can data-driven insights enhance our understanding of synaptic interactions and contextual awareness in the brain?
