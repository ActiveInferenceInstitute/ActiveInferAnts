# PhD Dissertation Plan: Transposing Health Care to the Fabric Industry and Arts

## Executive Summary
This dissertation explores the innovative fusion of health care principles with practices in the fabric industry and arts, proposing a transformative framework that enhances consumer engagement, sustainability, and quality assurance. By analyzing the core tenets of patient-centered care, service quality, and technology integration from health care, this research aims to revolutionize fabric design and production. The findings are expected to have significant implications for both industries, fostering interdisciplinary collaboration and paving the way for new sub-disciplines focused on consumer experience and sustainable practices.

## Introduction

### Background of the Shifted Domain
The Shifted Domain represents a novel intersection of health care and the fabric industry, where principles of patient-centered care are applied to enhance consumer experiences in textile design and production. This domain shift acknowledges the growing consumer demand for personalized products and sustainable practices, paralleling trends in health care that prioritize individual needs and environmental responsibility.

### Significance and Novelty of the Research
This research is significant as it challenges traditional paradigms within both industries, suggesting that the application of health care methodologies can lead to innovative practices in fabric design. The novelty lies in the systematic transposition of health care principles to the fabric industry, creating a new lexicon and framework for understanding consumer experiences.

### Overarching Research Questions and Objectives
1. How can patient-centered care principles enhance consumer engagement in the fabric industry?
2. What sustainable practices from health care can be integrated into textile production?
3. How does the application of technology in health care inform innovations in fabric design?

## Literature Review

### Historical Context of the Original Domains
- **Health Care**: The evolution from paternalistic care models to patient-centered approaches emphasizing quality, safety, and technology.
- **Fabric Industry**: The transition from traditional craftsmanship to modern industrial production, highlighting the cultural significance of textiles.

### Current State of Knowledge in Both Fields
- **Health Care**: Emphasis on patient experience, technology integration, and continuous improvement.
- **Fabric Industry**: Growing demand for sustainable practices amidst challenges posed by fast fashion.

### Gaps and Opportunities Presented by the Shifted Domain
- Lack of consumer engagement frameworks in the fabric industry.
- Opportunities for interdisciplinary collaboration to enhance sustainability and quality.

## Theoretical Framework

### Foundational Theories from Original Domains
- **Patient-Centered Care Theory**: Focuses on individual needs and experiences.
- **Service Quality Models**: Emphasize the importance of meeting consumer expectations.

### New Theoretical Constructs Emerging from the Shift
- **Consumer-Centric Fabric Design**: A model that prioritizes consumer involvement in the design process.
- **Holistic Fabric Experience**: A framework that encompasses emotional and cultural aspects of textile interactions.

### Proposed Integrated Theoretical Model
An integrated model that combines health care service quality metrics with consumer experience frameworks in the fabric industry, facilitating a comprehensive understanding of quality and engagement.
