# PhD Dissertation Plan: Transposing William Blake’s Life and Work into the Context of Cognitive Security

## Executive Summary
This dissertation aims to explore the innovative fusion of William Blake's artistic and philosophical insights with contemporary cognitive security practices. By analyzing Blake's concepts of imagination, contraries, and visionary experiences, this research will propose a new framework for enhancing cognitive resilience against misinformation and cognitive manipulation. The significance of this study lies in its potential to revolutionize cognitive security training and education through creative approaches, ultimately contributing to a more informed and resilient society.

## Introduction

### Background of the Shifted Domain
The Shifted Domain represents a unique intersection of literary and artistic exploration with the urgent need for cognitive security in a rapidly evolving digital landscape. William Blake's life and work offer profound insights into human cognition, imagination, and the dualities of experience, which can be leveraged to address contemporary challenges in cognitive security.

### Significance and Novelty of the Research
This research is novel in its approach to cognitive security, proposing that imaginative and artistic methodologies can enhance understanding and resilience against cognitive threats. By integrating Blake's philosophy with cognitive security principles, the study aims to develop innovative strategies that engage individuals on emotional and creative levels.

### Overarching Research Questions and Objectives
1. How can Blake's concepts of imagination and contraries inform cognitive security practices?
2. What innovative strategies can be developed to enhance cognitive resilience through artistic engagement?
3. How can the integration of Blake's philosophy into cognitive security education transform current pedagogical approaches?
