# PhD Dissertation Plan: Transposing the Free Energy Principle and Active Inference into Blockchain Technology

## Executive Summary
This dissertation aims to explore the innovative intersection of the Free Energy Principle (FEP) and blockchain technology, proposing a transformative framework that integrates concepts from cognitive science with decentralized systems. The research will investigate how predictive modeling and active inference can enhance blockchain governance, transaction processing, and smart contract functionality. By establishing a comprehensive theoretical and practical foundation, this dissertation seeks to contribute significantly to both fields, offering novel insights and applications that can revolutionize our understanding of complex adaptive systems.

## Introduction
### Background of the Shifted Domain
The Free Energy Principle provides a robust theoretical framework for understanding biological and cognitive processes, while blockchain technology offers a decentralized model for secure and transparent transactions. The convergence of these two domains presents a unique opportunity to enhance the adaptability and efficiency of blockchain systems through principles of predictive coding and active inference.

### Significance and Novelty of the Research
This research is significant as it not only bridges two distinct fields but also proposes new methodologies and frameworks that can lead to more resilient decentralized systems. The novelty lies in applying cognitive and biological principles to technological governance and transaction processing, which has not been extensively explored in existing literature.

### Overarching Research Questions and Objectives
1. How can the principles of active inference be integrated into blockchain governance models to enhance adaptability and resilience?
2. In what ways can predictive modeling improve the functionality and reliability of smart contracts?
3. What role does variational free energy play in optimizing transaction processing within blockchain networks?
