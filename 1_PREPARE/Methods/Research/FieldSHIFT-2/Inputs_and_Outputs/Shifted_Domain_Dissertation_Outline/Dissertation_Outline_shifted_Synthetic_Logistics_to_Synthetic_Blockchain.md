# PhD Dissertation Plan: Transposing Logistics into Blockchain

## Executive Summary
This dissertation will explore the innovative intersection of logistics and blockchain technology, aiming to develop a comprehensive understanding of how blockchain can transform logistics operations. By investigating the integration of decentralized ledger technology into supply chain management, this research seeks to address inefficiencies, enhance transparency, and improve overall operational resilience. The potential impact of this study extends beyond academia, offering practical applications for industry stakeholders and contributing to the development of new frameworks for logistics management.

## Introduction
### Background of the Shifted Domain
The logistics industry has long been characterized by its reliance on traditional methods of managing the flow of goods and information. In contrast, blockchain technology offers a transformative approach to data integrity, security, and transparency. This dissertation will analyze how the fusion of these two domains can lead to significant advancements in supply chain management.

### Significance and Novelty of the Research
The research will fill critical gaps in existing literature by providing an integrated framework that combines logistics principles with blockchain capabilities. This novel approach not only addresses current industry challenges but also opens avenues for future exploration in both fields.

### Overarching Research Questions and Objectives
- How can blockchain technology enhance the efficiency and transparency of logistics operations?
- What are the potential barriers to implementing blockchain in logistics, and how can they be overcome?
- How can smart contracts and decentralized networks reshape traditional supply chain management?
- To develop a theoretical model that integrates logistics and blockchain concepts.
