# PhD Dissertation Plan: Bridging William Blake's Life and Work with Contemporary Education in the Arts and Humanities

## Executive Summary
This dissertation aims to explore the transformative potential of integrating William Blake's philosophies and artistic practices into contemporary education, particularly within the arts and humanities. By examining Blake's core principles—such as the importance of imagination, the coexistence of contraries, and spiritual vision—this research seeks to reimagine educational frameworks that prioritize creativity, interdisciplinary learning, and personal expression. The significance of this study lies in its potential to reshape educational paradigms, foster emotional intelligence, and cultivate innovative thinkers equipped to address complex societal challenges. Ultimately, this dissertation will provide a comprehensive roadmap for implementing these ideas in educational settings, offering insights that can revolutionize teaching and learning in the 21st century.

## Introduction

### Background of the Shifted Domain
The Shifted Domain represents a novel intersection between the life and work of William Blake, a seminal figure in the Romantic movement, and contemporary educational practices in the arts and humanities. Blake's emphasis on imagination, creativity, and spiritual engagement offers a rich framework for addressing current challenges in education, where analytical skills often overshadow creative expression.

### Significance and Novelty of the Research
This research is significant as it proposes a paradigm shift in educational approaches, advocating for a curriculum that values imaginative engagement and interdisciplinary learning. By drawing on Blake's insights, this dissertation seeks to demonstrate how the arts can foster critical thinking, empathy, and a deeper understanding of complex social issues, thereby enriching the educational landscape.

### Overarching Research Questions and Objectives
- How can William Blake's philosophies inform contemporary educational practices in the arts and humanities?
- What are the implications of prioritizing imaginative engagement in education for student outcomes?
- How can interdisciplinary approaches reshape the understanding of social issues among students?
