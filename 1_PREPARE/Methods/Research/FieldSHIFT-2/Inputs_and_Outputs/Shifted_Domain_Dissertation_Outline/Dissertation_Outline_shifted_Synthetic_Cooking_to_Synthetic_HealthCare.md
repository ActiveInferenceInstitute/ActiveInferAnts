# PhD Dissertation Plan: Transposing Culinary Concepts to Health Care

## Executive Summary
This dissertation explores the innovative intersection of culinary arts and health care, positing that principles from cuisine can transform patient care delivery. By establishing a framework that integrates culinary concepts into health care practices, this research aims to enhance patient engagement, satisfaction, and outcomes. The transformative potential of this Shifted Domain lies in its ability to foster a holistic, personalized approach to health care, bridging the gap between patient experiences and clinical interventions. The findings are expected to have significant implications for both culinary education and health care practices.

## Introduction
### Background of the Shifted Domain
The culinary arts and health care have traditionally been viewed as separate domains, each with its own set of principles, practices, and objectives. However, both fields share common goals: to enhance human experience and well-being. This dissertation investigates how culinary concepts such as flavor profiles, mise en place, and recipe development can be systematically transposed into health care settings to improve patient experiences and outcomes.

### Significance and Novelty of the Research
This research is significant as it represents a pioneering effort to merge culinary arts with health care, creating a novel framework that challenges conventional paradigms in both fields. By tapping into the rich traditions of cuisine, this dissertation seeks to redefine patient care, emphasizing personalization, engagement, and interdisciplinary collaboration.

### Overarching Research Questions and Objectives
- How can the concept of "patient flavor profiles" improve individual care plans?
- What impact does "care mise en place" have on treatment outcomes and patient satisfaction?
- How can interdisciplinary fusion care models enhance the effectiveness of treatment for chronic illnesses?
