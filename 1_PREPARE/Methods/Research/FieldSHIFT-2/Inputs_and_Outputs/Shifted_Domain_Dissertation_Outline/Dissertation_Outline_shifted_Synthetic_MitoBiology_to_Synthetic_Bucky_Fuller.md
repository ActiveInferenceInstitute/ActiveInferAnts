# PhD Dissertation Plan: Mitochondria to Buckminster Fuller’s Philosophy

## Executive Summary
This dissertation explores the innovative fusion of mitochondrial biology and Buckminster Fuller's philosophy, proposing a transformative framework for sustainable design and resource management. By investigating the parallels between mitochondrial processes and Fuller's holistic design principles, this research aims to develop practical applications that address contemporary global challenges such as climate change, resource depletion, and social inequity. The potential impact includes the establishment of new interdisciplinary fields, innovative urban designs, and sustainable practices that enhance societal resilience.

## Introduction

### Background of the Shifted Domain
The Shifted Domain represents a novel intersection between mitochondrial biology, which focuses on cellular energy production and efficiency, and Buckminster Fuller’s philosophy, which emphasizes holistic, sustainable design. This fusion offers a unique lens through which to address complex global challenges by drawing on biological principles to inform design and resource management strategies.

### Significance and Novelty of the Research
This research is significant as it bridges two previously distinct domains, offering fresh perspectives on sustainability and efficiency. By applying biological concepts to design thinking, the dissertation aims to create a new framework that can guide innovative solutions to pressing societal issues. The novelty lies in the transposition of biological principles into the context of design, leading to the emergence of new methodologies and practices.

### Overarching Research Questions and Objectives
- How can mitochondrial concepts inform sustainable design practices in urban environments?
- What are the implications of applying biological efficiency to resource management?
- How can interdisciplinary collaborations enhance the practical applications of this Shifted Domain?
