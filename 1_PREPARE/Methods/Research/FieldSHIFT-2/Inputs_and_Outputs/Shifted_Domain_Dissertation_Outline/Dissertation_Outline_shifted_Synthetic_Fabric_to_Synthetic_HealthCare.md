# PhD Dissertation Plan: Transposing the Fabric Industry and Arts into Healthcare

## Executive Summary
This dissertation aims to explore the innovative intersection of the fabric industry and healthcare, proposing a transformative framework that integrates principles of design, sustainability, and cultural sensitivity into patient care. By leveraging the creativity and methodologies of the fabric arts, this research seeks to enhance patient experiences and outcomes while promoting environmentally sustainable practices within healthcare settings. The potential impact of this work extends beyond individual patient care, offering insights into interdisciplinary collaboration and the future of healthcare innovation.

## Introduction
### Background of the Shifted Domain
The fabric industry, known for its creativity, craftsmanship, and cultural expression, has evolved significantly, adopting sustainable practices and technological advancements. Conversely, healthcare has shifted towards patient-centered care, emphasizing accessibility, engagement, and quality. This dissertation investigates how the principles of the fabric industry can be transposed into healthcare, offering a novel approach to patient care.

### Significance and Novelty of the Research
This research is significant as it bridges two distinct fields, creating a unique interdisciplinary approach that addresses pressing challenges in healthcare, such as patient satisfaction and sustainability. By integrating design principles from the fabric industry, this work proposes innovative solutions that can reshape patient experiences and outcomes.

### Overarching Research Questions and Objectives
- How can principles of textile design enhance personalized patient care?
- In what ways can sustainable practices from the fabric industry be integrated into healthcare?
- What role does cultural sensitivity play in improving patient outcomes?
- To develop a comprehensive framework that combines insights from both fields to inform future healthcare practices.
