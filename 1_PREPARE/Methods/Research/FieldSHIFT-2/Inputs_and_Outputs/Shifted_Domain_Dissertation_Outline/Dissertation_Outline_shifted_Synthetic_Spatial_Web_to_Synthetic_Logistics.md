# PhD Dissertation Plan: Transposing Concepts from the Spatial Web to Logistics

## Executive Summary
This dissertation aims to explore the innovative intersection of the Spatial Web and Logistics, proposing a comprehensive framework for integrating real-time data utilization, augmented reality, and smart infrastructure into logistics operations. By addressing key challenges such as last-mile delivery efficiency, supply chain visibility, and sustainability, this research will pave the way for enhanced operational performance and customer engagement. The anticipated impact of this work extends beyond logistics, offering insights that can transform urban planning, supply chain management, and technology adoption across various sectors.

## Introduction

### Background of the Shifted Domain
The Spatial Web represents a revolutionary approach to merging physical and digital environments, utilizing technologies such as IoT, AR, and VR. Conversely, logistics has traditionally focused on the efficient movement and storage of goods. The convergence of these domains presents unique opportunities for innovation and efficiency.

### Significance and Novelty of the Research
This research is significant as it not only bridges two previously distinct fields but also addresses pressing challenges in logistics through the lens of advanced technologies. The novelty lies in the application of Spatial Web principles to enhance logistics operations, providing a fresh perspective on age-old problems.

### Overarching Research Questions and Objectives
- How can Spatial Web technologies enhance logistics efficiency and customer engagement?
- What are the implications of integrating digital twins and IoT in supply chain management?
- How can augmented reality transform last-mile delivery processes?
