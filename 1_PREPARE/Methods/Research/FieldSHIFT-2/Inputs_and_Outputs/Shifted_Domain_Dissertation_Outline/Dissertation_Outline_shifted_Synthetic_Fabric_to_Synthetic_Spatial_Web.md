# PhD Dissertation Plan: Transposing the Fabric Industry and Arts into the Spatial Web

## Executive Summary
This dissertation explores the innovative fusion of the fabric industry and arts with the Spatial Web, proposing a transformative framework that integrates traditional textile practices with cutting-edge digital technologies. By examining the intersections between these domains, the research aims to uncover novel applications, enhance cultural representation, and promote sustainability. The findings will have significant implications for both industries, paving the way for new practices, educational paradigms, and interdisciplinary collaborations.

## Introduction
### Background of the Shifted Domain
The fabric industry encompasses a rich tapestry of cultural heritage and modern innovation, while the Spatial Web represents a frontier of digital interaction and connectivity. This dissertation seeks to understand how these distinct domains can converge, creating a new landscape for textile design, production, and consumption.

### Significance and Novelty of the Research
This research is significant as it addresses the urgent need for sustainability and innovation in the fabric industry while leveraging the capabilities of the Spatial Web. The novelty lies in the interdisciplinary approach that combines artistry with technology, fostering a deeper understanding of textiles in a digital context.

### Overarching Research Questions and Objectives
- How can augmented reality and smart textiles redefine consumer engagement in the fabric industry?
- What are the cultural implications of integrating digital narratives with traditional textile practices?
- How can sustainable practices be enhanced through the application of blockchain technology in textile supply chains?
