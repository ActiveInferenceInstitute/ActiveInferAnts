# PhD Dissertation Plan: Transposing Prediction Matter Expertise (PME) into Cognitive Security

## Executive Summary
This dissertation aims to explore the innovative integration of Prediction Matter Expertise (PME) into the field of Cognitive Security. By synthesizing methodologies and principles from both domains, the research will develop new frameworks and practices to enhance resilience against cognitive threats such as misinformation and disinformation. The significance of this work lies in its potential to revolutionize cognitive security strategies, offering proactive measures based on predictive analytics and interdisciplinary collaboration. The findings will contribute to both academic discourse and practical applications, impacting education, technology, and policy-making in the realm of cognitive security.

## Introduction

### Background of the Shifted Domain
The Shifted Domain represents a transformative intersection between PME and Cognitive Security, where predictive analytics and interdisciplinary insights are harnessed to combat cognitive threats. As society faces increasing challenges from misinformation and cognitive manipulation, leveraging PME's analytical capabilities becomes crucial.

### Significance and Novelty of the Research
This research is novel in its approach to merging predictive methodologies with cognitive security frameworks. By exploring how PME can inform cognitive resilience, the dissertation will fill a critical gap in understanding and addressing cognitive vulnerabilities in the information age.

### Overarching Research Questions and Objectives
- How can PME methodologies enhance the effectiveness of cognitive security measures?
- What novel training programs can be developed to improve resilience against cognitive threats?
- In what ways can predictive analytics inform the identification and mitigation of misinformation?
