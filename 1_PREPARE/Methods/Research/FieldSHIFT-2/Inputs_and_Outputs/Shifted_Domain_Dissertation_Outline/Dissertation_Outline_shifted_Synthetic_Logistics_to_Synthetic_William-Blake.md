# PhD Dissertation Plan: Transposing Logistics Principles onto the Life and Work of William Blake

## Executive Summary
This dissertation aims to explore the innovative intersection between logistics and the artistic life of William Blake. By applying logistics principles—such as efficiency, coordination, visibility, flexibility, and sustainability—to Blake's creative processes, this research seeks to uncover new insights into the production, dissemination, and reception of his work. The significance of this study lies in its potential to redefine our understanding of creativity, enhance educational practices, and influence contemporary artistic methodologies. Ultimately, this research endeavors to contribute to both the fields of logistics and art, fostering interdisciplinary dialogue and collaboration.

## Introduction

### Background of the Shifted Domain
The fusion of logistics and the life and work of William Blake represents a novel approach to understanding creativity and artistic production. Logistics, traditionally concerned with the flow of goods and services, offers a structured framework that can illuminate the intricacies of Blake's artistic endeavors.

### Significance and Novelty of the Research
This research is significant as it challenges conventional boundaries between art and logistics, proposing that principles from logistics can enhance our understanding of artistic creativity. The novelty lies in the application of logistics as a lens through which to analyze Blake’s work, offering fresh interpretations and methodologies.

### Overarching Research Questions and Objectives
1. How can logistics principles enhance the production and dissemination of Blake's works?
2. What are the implications of viewing Blake's artistic processes through a logistical lens?
3. How can this interdisciplinary approach contribute to the fields of logistics and art?
