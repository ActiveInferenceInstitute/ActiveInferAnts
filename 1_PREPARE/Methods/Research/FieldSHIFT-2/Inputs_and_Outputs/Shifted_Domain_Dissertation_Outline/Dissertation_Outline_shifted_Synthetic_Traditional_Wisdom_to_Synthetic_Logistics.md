# PhD Dissertation Plan: Integrating Traditional Knowledge into Logistics

## Executive Summary
This dissertation explores the transformative potential of integrating Traditional Knowledge (TK) into the logistics domain, creating a Shifted Domain that emphasizes sustainability, community engagement, and cultural sensitivity. By examining the interplay between these two fields, this research aims to develop innovative logistics practices that not only enhance operational efficiency but also foster ecological stewardship and social responsibility. The findings are expected to provide a comprehensive framework for reimagining logistics, influencing both academic discourse and practical applications in the industry.

## Introduction

### Background of the Shifted Domain
The integration of Traditional Knowledge into logistics represents a significant shift in how supply chains can be conceptualized and managed. Traditional Knowledge, rooted in the practices and beliefs of indigenous and local communities, offers valuable insights into sustainable resource management and community-centered approaches. On the other hand, logistics, traditionally focused on efficiency and optimization, is increasingly recognizing the need for sustainable practices that consider environmental and social impacts.

### Significance and Novelty of the Research
This research is significant as it bridges two previously distinct fields, offering a novel perspective on logistics that prioritizes sustainability and community involvement. By leveraging TK, the dissertation aims to challenge conventional logistics paradigms and propose innovative frameworks that can lead to more resilient supply chains.

### Overarching Research Questions and Objectives
1. How can Traditional Knowledge inform and enhance logistics practices?
2. What are the potential benefits and challenges of integrating TK into logistics?
3. How does the integration of TK influence community engagement and sustainability in supply chains?
4. What new theoretical constructs emerge from this Shifted Domain?
