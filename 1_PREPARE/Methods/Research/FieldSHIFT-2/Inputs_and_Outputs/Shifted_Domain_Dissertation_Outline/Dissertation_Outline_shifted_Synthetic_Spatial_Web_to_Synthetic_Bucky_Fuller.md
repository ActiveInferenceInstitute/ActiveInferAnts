# PhD Dissertation Plan: Transposing the Spatial Web into the Context of Buckminster Fuller’s Philosophy

## Executive Summary
This dissertation aims to explore the innovative intersection of the Spatial Web and Buckminster Fuller’s philosophy, creating a new framework for understanding urban design, sustainability, and resource management. By integrating advanced technologies such as AR, VR, IoT, and AI with Fuller's principles of holistic design and sustainability, this research seeks to generate novel hypotheses, practical applications, and interdisciplinary collaborations. The potential impact includes transforming urban planning practices, enhancing community engagement, and fostering sustainable resource management on a global scale.

## Introduction

### Background of the Shifted Domain
The Spatial Web represents a significant evolution in how we interact with our environments, merging digital and physical realities through advanced technologies. Concurrently, Buckminster Fuller’s philosophy emphasizes sustainable design and holistic systems thinking. This dissertation will investigate how these two domains can inform and enhance one another, leading to innovative solutions for contemporary challenges.

### Significance and Novelty of the Research
This research is significant because it unites two previously distinct fields, offering a fresh perspective on urban design and sustainability. The novelty lies in applying Fuller's systemic thinking to the technological advancements of the Spatial Web, creating a comprehensive framework that addresses pressing global issues.

### Overarching Research Questions and Objectives
1. How can the principles of the Spatial Web be integrated into urban planning through Fuller’s philosophy?
2. What are the implications of this integration for sustainable design practices?
3. How can this new framework enhance community engagement and resource management?
