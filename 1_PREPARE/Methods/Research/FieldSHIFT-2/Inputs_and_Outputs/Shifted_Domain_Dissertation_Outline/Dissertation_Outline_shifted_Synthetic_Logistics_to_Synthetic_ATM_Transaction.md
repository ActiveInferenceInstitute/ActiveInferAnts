# PhD Dissertation Plan: Transposing Logistics into ATM Transactions

## Executive Summary
This dissertation explores the innovative integration of logistics principles into the realm of ATM transactions, a Shifted Domain that leverages the efficiency, optimization, and customer-centric focus of logistics to enhance ATM operations. The research aims to develop new theories and frameworks, such as the ATM Cash Flow Optimization Theory and the User-Centric ATM Design Framework, which will provide a comprehensive understanding of how logistics can revolutionize ATM networks. The potential impact of this research includes improved operational efficiency, enhanced user experience, and sustainable practices in the banking sector, paving the way for future advancements in both fields.

## Introduction

### Background of the Shifted Domain
The Shifted Domain merges the fields of logistics and ATM transactions, two areas that have traditionally operated independently. Logistics focuses on the efficient movement and storage of goods, while ATM transactions involve self-service banking operations. As the banking industry faces challenges related to efficiency, security, and user satisfaction, the principles of logistics offer valuable insights and solutions.

### Significance and Novelty of the Research
This research is significant because it identifies and articulates the synergies between logistics and ATM transactions. By applying logistics principles to ATM operations, this dissertation aims to address existing inefficiencies and enhance customer satisfaction. The novelty lies in the creation of new theoretical constructs and practical applications that bridge these two domains.

### Overarching Research Questions and Objectives
- How can logistics principles be systematically integrated into ATM transaction processes?
- What new theories and frameworks can emerge from this integration?
- What are the implications for operational efficiency, user experience, and sustainability in ATM networks?
