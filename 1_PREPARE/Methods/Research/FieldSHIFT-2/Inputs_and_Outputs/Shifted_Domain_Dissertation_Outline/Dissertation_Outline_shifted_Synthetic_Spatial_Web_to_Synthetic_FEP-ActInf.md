# Dissertation Plan: Integrating the Spatial Web into the Free Energy Principle and Active Inference

## Executive Summary
This dissertation aims to explore the transformative integration of the Spatial Web—a convergence of physical and digital spaces—into the Free Energy Principle (FEP) and Active Inference frameworks. By examining the synergies between these two domains, the research will address how this fusion can enhance predictive modeling, contextual adaptation, and understanding of organism-environment interactions. The findings are expected to have significant implications across multiple fields, including cognitive science, artificial intelligence, urban planning, and healthcare, ultimately contributing to more effective adaptive systems and innovative technologies.

## Introduction
### Background of the Shifted Domain
The Spatial Web represents a new frontier in which digital and physical interactions are seamlessly integrated through technologies like AR, VR, IoT, and Edge Computing. The Free Energy Principle offers a theoretical foundation for understanding how adaptive systems maintain their integrity by minimizing surprise. This dissertation will investigate how these domains can inform and enhance each other.

### Significance and Novelty of the Research
This research is novel as it bridges two historically distinct fields, creating a unique interdisciplinary approach that could redefine our understanding of cognition and interaction in complex environments. The implications of this integration are profound, potentially leading to new insights in adaptive systems, enhanced AI applications, and improved human-computer interaction.

### Overarching Research Questions and Objectives
1. How can spatial data enhance predictive models within the Free Energy Principle?
2. In what ways can Active Inference be adapted to include spatial contexts and real-time feedback?
3. What new theoretical constructs emerge from integrating the Spatial Web with the FEP?
