# PhD Dissertation Plan: Transposing Entomology into Ecosystem Biology

## Executive Summary
This dissertation aims to explore the transformative potential of integrating entomological principles into ecosystem biology, creating a novel framework that enhances our understanding of ecosystems and their dynamics. By investigating the parallels between insect life cycles and ecosystem succession, the roles of functional diversity, and the implications of social structures in insect communities, this research will provide a comprehensive analysis of how these insights can revolutionize ecological study and practice. The anticipated outcomes include the development of new theoretical constructs, practical applications for biodiversity conservation, and a roadmap for future interdisciplinary research.

## Introduction

### Background of the Shifted Domain
The Shifted Domain represents a groundbreaking intersection between entomology—the study of insects—and ecosystem biology, which examines the interactions between organisms and their environments. This integration is crucial as it addresses the pressing need for innovative approaches to understanding biodiversity and ecosystem resilience in the face of global environmental challenges.

### Significance and Novelty of the Research
This research is significant as it challenges traditional paradigms within both fields, proposing a unified framework that recognizes the essential roles insects play in ecosystem functioning. The novelty lies in the systematic transposition of entomological concepts to elucidate ecosystem dynamics, providing fresh insights into biodiversity, resilience, and conservation strategies.

### Overarching Research Questions and Objectives
- How can entomological principles inform our understanding of ecosystem dynamics?
- What new theoretical constructs can emerge from this integration?
- How can these insights be applied to enhance biodiversity conservation and ecosystem management?
