# PhD Dissertation Plan: Transformative Domain Shift from Cuisine to Culinary Innovation and Sustainability

## Executive Summary
This dissertation explores the innovative fusion of traditional cuisine with contemporary culinary innovation and sustainability. By analyzing the intersections of cultural heritage and modern practices, the research aims to develop a comprehensive framework that enhances culinary practices while addressing pressing global challenges such as food security, health, and environmental sustainability. This study will not only contribute to the academic discourse in culinary arts but also provide practical applications and guidelines for professionals in the field, ultimately fostering a more sustainable and innovative culinary landscape.

## Introduction

### Background of the Shifted Domain
The Shifted Domain represents the convergence of traditional culinary practices (Domain A: Cuisine) and modern culinary innovation focused on sustainability (Domain B: Culinary Innovation and Sustainability). This intersection allows for the transformation of culinary techniques and philosophies, creating a new paradigm that respects cultural heritage while embracing contemporary challenges.

### Significance and Novelty of the Research
This research is significant in its potential to redefine culinary practices by integrating sustainability into the core of culinary innovation. It addresses gaps in existing literature regarding the practical application of traditional culinary techniques in modern contexts and offers a fresh perspective on how cultural practices can inform sustainable innovations.

### Overarching Research Questions and Objectives
1. How can traditional culinary techniques be adapted to promote sustainability in contemporary culinary practices?
2. What role does culinary innovation play in reducing food waste and enhancing flavor profiles?
3. How can the integration of sustainability into culinary education transform the culinary industry?

## Literature Review

### Historical Context of the Original Domains
- **Cuisine:** Examination of the evolution of culinary practices through historical events, cultural exchanges, and technological advancements.
- **Culinary Innovation and Sustainability:** Overview of the rise of sustainable practices in response to environmental concerns and health challenges.

### Current State of Knowledge in Both Fields
- Analysis of contemporary culinary trends, including farm-to-table movements, health consciousness, and the impact of globalization on culinary practices.

### Gaps and Opportunities Presented by the Shifted Domain
- Identification of a lack of interdisciplinary research connecting traditional culinary practices with modern sustainability efforts, presenting an opportunity for innovative contributions.

## Theoretical Framework

### Foundational Theories from Original Domains
- **Cultural Theory:** Understanding cuisine as a reflection of cultural identity and social structures.
- **Sustainability Theory:** Principles of environmental stewardship and sustainable practices in food systems.

### New Theoretical Constructs Emerging from the Shift
- **Culinary Stewardship:** A concept that emphasizes the responsibility of culinary professionals to advocate for sustainable practices.
- **Flavor Sustainability:** A framework for maximizing flavor while minimizing environmental impact.

### Proposed Integrated Theoretical Model
- Development of a model that connects traditional culinary practices with sustainable innovations, illustrating the potential for cultural adaptation and community engagement.

## Methodology

### Research Design Overview
- A mixed-methods approach combining qualitative and quantitative research to explore the intersections of cuisine and sustainability.

### Data Collection Methods
- **Surveys and Interviews:** Gathering insights from culinary professionals, educators, and consumers regarding perceptions of sustainability in culinary practices.
- **Culinary Workshops:** Conducting hands-on sessions to create and evaluate sustainable dishes.

### Analytical Approaches
- Qualitative analysis of interview data and thematic analysis of workshop outcomes.
- Quantitative analysis of survey data to identify trends and correlations.

### Ethical Considerations
- Ensuring informed consent from participants and adherence to ethical guidelines in culinary research.
