# PhD Dissertation Plan: Transposing Buckminster Fuller’s Principles to ATM Transactions

## Executive Summary
This dissertation aims to explore the innovative fusion of Buckminster Fuller’s design principles with the operational frameworks of Automated Teller Machines (ATMs). By leveraging Fuller's holistic approach, ephemeralization, tensegrity, and synergetics, this research proposes a transformative ATM framework that enhances user experience, operational efficiency, and sustainability. The significance of this work lies in its potential to revolutionize the banking sector, creating ATMs that serve as multifunctional hubs within communities while addressing pressing challenges such as resource efficiency and technological adaptation. The anticipated impact extends beyond banking, influencing broader discussions on sustainable design practices and interdisciplinary collaboration.

## Introduction
### Background of the Shifted Domain
The convergence of Buckminster Fuller’s design philosophies and contemporary ATM technology represents a novel domain ripe for exploration. Fuller’s principles advocate for a systemic understanding of global challenges, emphasizing sustainability and efficiency, while ATMs have evolved into essential components of modern banking infrastructure. This dissertation seeks to understand how these two domains can inform and enhance each other.

### Significance and Novelty of the Research
This research is significant due to its potential to redefine the role of ATMs in society. By applying Fuller's principles, the study aims to create a new paradigm for ATM design that prioritizes user experience, environmental sustainability, and community engagement. The novelty lies in the systematic transposition of theoretical constructs from a design science perspective to a banking technology context.

### Overarching Research Questions and Objectives
- How can Buckminster Fuller’s principles be systematically applied to enhance ATM design and functionality?
- What are the implications of this transposition for user experience, operational efficiency, and sustainability in banking?
- What new theoretical constructs emerge from this interdisciplinary approach, and how can they inform future research and practice?
