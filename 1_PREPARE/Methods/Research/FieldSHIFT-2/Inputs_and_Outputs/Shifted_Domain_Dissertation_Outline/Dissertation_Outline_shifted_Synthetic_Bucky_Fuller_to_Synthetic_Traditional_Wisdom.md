# PhD Dissertation Plan: Transposing Buckminster Fuller’s Concepts to Traditional Knowledge

## Executive Summary
This dissertation explores the innovative integration of Buckminster Fuller's design principles with traditional knowledge systems, aiming to create a transformative framework for sustainable practices. The significance of this research lies in its potential to bridge modern scientific approaches with indigenous wisdom, fostering environmental stewardship and resilience. By investigating the synergies between these two domains, the dissertation seeks to contribute to sustainable development, community empowerment, and the preservation of cultural heritage.

## Introduction
### Background of the Shifted Domain
The Shifted Domain represents the intersection of Buckminster Fuller’s design science and traditional knowledge systems. Fuller’s holistic approach to problem-solving and sustainability aligns with the ecological understanding inherent in traditional knowledge, creating opportunities for collaborative innovations.

### Significance and Novelty of the Research
This research is significant as it not only honors traditional knowledge but also revitalizes it through the lens of modern design principles. The novelty lies in the potential for creating a new interdisciplinary framework that can address contemporary global challenges such as climate change, resource scarcity, and cultural erosion.

### Overarching Research Questions and Objectives
1. How can Fuller's design principles enhance traditional knowledge systems for sustainable practices?
2. What are the emergent properties of integrating these two domains in community resource management?
3. How can this integration inform policy and educational frameworks for sustainability?
