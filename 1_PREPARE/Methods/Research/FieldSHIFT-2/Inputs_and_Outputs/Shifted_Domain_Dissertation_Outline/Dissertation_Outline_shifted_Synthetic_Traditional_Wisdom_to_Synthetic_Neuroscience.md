# Comprehensive PhD Dissertation Plan: Transposing Traditional Knowledge into Neuroscience

## Executive Summary
This dissertation aims to explore the transformative potential of integrating Traditional Knowledge (TK) into the field of Neuroscience. By examining the isomorphisms between these two domains, the research will propose a new interdisciplinary framework that emphasizes holistic understanding, cultural influences on cognition, and community engagement. The significance of this research lies in its potential to revolutionize our understanding of cognitive processes, enhance mental health interventions, and foster sustainable practices. This work will pave the way for novel applications in education, policy, and community well-being.

## Introduction

### Background of the Shifted Domain
Traditional Knowledge has long been marginalized in scientific discourse, yet it embodies rich insights into sustainability, community well-being, and ecological stewardship. Neuroscience, with its focus on the biological underpinnings of behavior, often overlooks the cultural and social contexts that shape cognition. This dissertation seeks to bridge these domains, creating a comprehensive understanding of how TK can inform and enrich neuroscientific inquiry.

### Significance and Novelty of the Research
The integration of TK into neuroscience presents a novel approach to understanding cognitive processes, challenging reductionist methodologies and emphasizing the importance of cultural context. This research is significant as it not only honors indigenous wisdom but also addresses contemporary challenges such as mental health crises and environmental degradation.

### Overarching Research Questions and Objectives
1. How does Traditional Knowledge influence cognitive processes and brain function?
2. What new theoretical constructs emerge from the integration of TK into neuroscience?
3. How can participatory methods from TK enhance neuroscientific research and community engagement?
