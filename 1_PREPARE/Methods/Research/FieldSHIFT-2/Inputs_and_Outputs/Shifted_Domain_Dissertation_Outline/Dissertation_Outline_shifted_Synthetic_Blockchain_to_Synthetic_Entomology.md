# PhD Dissertation Plan: Integrating Blockchain Technology into Entomology

## Executive Summary
This dissertation aims to explore the transformative potential of integrating blockchain technology into the field of entomology. By leveraging blockchain's decentralized, immutable ledger system, the research seeks to enhance biodiversity monitoring, improve pest management strategies, and foster interdisciplinary collaboration. The significance of this study lies in its ability to address current challenges in insect research and management while paving the way for innovative solutions that promote sustainability and ecosystem health. The anticipated impact extends beyond entomology, influencing agricultural practices, conservation efforts, and the broader scientific community.

## Introduction
### Background of the Shifted Domain
The convergence of blockchain technology and entomology represents a novel interdisciplinary approach that merges digital innovation with biological research. Blockchain, initially developed for cryptocurrency, offers unique features such as transparency, security, and decentralized data management, which can be effectively applied to the study of insects.

### Significance and Novelty of the Research
The integration of these two domains is groundbreaking, as it addresses critical issues in entomology, such as declining insect populations and the need for innovative pest management strategies. This research is positioned at the intersection of technology and biology, offering new methodologies for data collection and analysis that can enhance ecological understanding and conservation efforts.

### Overarching Research Questions and Objectives
1. How can blockchain technology improve the accuracy and transparency of insect population monitoring?
2. In what ways can smart contracts automate and enhance integrated pest management strategies?
3. What interdisciplinary frameworks can be developed to facilitate collaboration between blockchain developers and entomologists?

## Literature Review
### Historical Context of the Original Domains
#### Blockchain
Blockchain technology emerged with the advent of Bitcoin in 2009, evolving to encompass various applications across multiple sectors, including finance, supply chain management, and governance.

#### Entomology
Entomology has evolved from basic taxonomy to a more integrated discipline that considers ecological interactions, evolutionary biology, and applied research in agriculture.

### Current State of Knowledge in Both Fields
- **Blockchain**: Current trends focus on integration with AI and IoT, sustainability, and regulatory frameworks.
- **Entomology**: Key areas include biodiversity, pest management, and insect physiology, with ongoing challenges related to population decline and ecological understanding.

### Gaps and Opportunities Presented by the Shifted Domain
- Limited application of blockchain in biological research.
- Need for innovative solutions to monitor insect populations and manage pests effectively.
