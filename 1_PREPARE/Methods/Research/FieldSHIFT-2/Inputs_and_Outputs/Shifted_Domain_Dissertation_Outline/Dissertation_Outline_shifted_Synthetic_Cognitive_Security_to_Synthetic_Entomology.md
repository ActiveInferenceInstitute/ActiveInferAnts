# Executive Summary

This dissertation explores the innovative fusion of cognitive security and entomology, proposing a transformative framework that enhances the integrity of entomological research and public understanding of insects. The significance of this research lies in its potential to address misinformation and cognitive biases that impact both scientific inquiry and societal perceptions of insects. By establishing a comprehensive roadmap for integrating cognitive security principles into entomology, this dissertation aims to foster interdisciplinary collaboration, improve data integrity, and promote effective conservation strategies. The anticipated impact is profound, as it not only enriches both fields but also contributes to broader ecological sustainability and societal trust in scientific information.

# Introduction

## Background of the Shifted Domain

The intersection of cognitive security and entomology represents a novel domain that addresses critical challenges in both fields. Cognitive security focuses on protecting cognitive processes from manipulation and misinformation, while entomology studies the vital roles insects play in ecosystems. This dissertation aims to explore how principles of cognitive security can be applied to enhance the integrity of entomological research and public perceptions of insects.

## Significance and Novelty of the Research

This research is significant as it addresses the growing concerns over misinformation in scientific discourse, particularly regarding insect populations, which are crucial for biodiversity and ecosystem health. The novelty lies in creating a framework that integrates cognitive security principles into entomological practices, thereby promoting accurate data reporting and improving public understanding of insect roles.

## Overarching Research Questions and Objectives

1. How can cognitive security principles be applied to enhance the integrity of entomological research?
2. What cognitive biases affect public perceptions of insects, and how can they be mitigated?
3. What are the implications of integrating cognitive security into entomological education and practice?
