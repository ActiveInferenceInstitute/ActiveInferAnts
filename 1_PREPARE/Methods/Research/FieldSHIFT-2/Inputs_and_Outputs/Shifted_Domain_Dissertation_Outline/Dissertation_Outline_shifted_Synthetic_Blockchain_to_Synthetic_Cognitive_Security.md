# Executive Summary

This dissertation proposes a comprehensive exploration of the innovative Shifted Domain that merges Blockchain technology with Cognitive Security. By leveraging the core principles of decentralization, immutability, transparency, and security inherent in Blockchain, this research aims to address the growing challenges posed by misinformation and cognitive threats. The significance of this study lies in its potential to revolutionize how individuals and organizations verify information, thereby enhancing trust and accountability in digital communication. This dissertation will provide a rigorous framework for understanding and implementing Blockchain solutions in the cognitive security landscape, ultimately contributing to a more informed and resilient society.

# Introduction

## Background of the Shifted Domain

The Shifted Domain combines the foundational aspects of Blockchain technology—originating from its inception with Bitcoin in 2009—with the emerging field of Cognitive Security, which focuses on protecting individuals and organizations from cognitive threats such as misinformation and social engineering attacks. This fusion presents a unique opportunity to leverage Blockchain's inherent capabilities to enhance information integrity and security.

## Significance and Novelty of the Research

This research is significant as it addresses critical gaps in both fields. The integration of Blockchain into Cognitive Security offers a novel approach to mitigating misinformation and enhancing digital literacy, potentially transforming how information is verified and trusted. By establishing a decentralized verification system, this dissertation aims to create a paradigm shift in the way cognitive threats are managed.

## Overarching Research Questions and Objectives

1. How can Blockchain technology enhance Cognitive Security measures against misinformation and social engineering?
2. What are the theoretical implications of merging Blockchain and Cognitive Security?
3. What practical applications can be developed to operationalize this integration?
