# PhD Dissertation Plan: Transformative Domain Shift from Buckminster Fuller to Cognitive Security

## Executive Summary
This dissertation aims to explore the transformative potential of merging the principles of Buckminster Fuller’s design philosophy with the emerging field of cognitive security. By integrating Fuller's holistic thinking, systems design, and anticipatory methodologies with contemporary cognitive security challenges, this research seeks to develop a comprehensive framework that enhances resilience against misinformation and cognitive manipulation. Through interdisciplinary collaboration and innovative research methodologies, the dissertation will contribute significantly to both fields, offering practical applications and theoretical advancements that address pressing global challenges.

## Introduction
### Background of the Shifted Domain
The Shifted Domain represents an innovative intersection between Buckminster Fuller’s design science principles and the contemporary challenges of cognitive security. Fuller’s emphasis on holistic thinking, sustainable design, and systemic solutions provides a unique lens through which to view and address cognitive vulnerabilities in an increasingly complex information landscape.

### Significance and Novelty of the Research
This research is significant as it not only revitalizes Fuller's legacy by applying his principles to modern challenges but also positions cognitive security as a necessary interdisciplinary field. By proposing an integrated framework that combines design science with cognitive resilience, this dissertation aims to fill critical gaps in both fields and pave the way for future research and practical applications.

### Overarching Research Questions and Objectives
1. How can the principles of Buckminster Fuller be applied to enhance cognitive security frameworks?
2. What novel theoretical constructs emerge from the integration of design science and cognitive security?
3. In what ways can interdisciplinary approaches foster resilience against cognitive threats?
