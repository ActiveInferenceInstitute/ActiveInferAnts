# PhD Dissertation Plan: Transposing Logistics to RxInfer.jl

## Executive Summary
This dissertation aims to explore the innovative intersection between logistics and probabilistic programming through RxInfer.jl, a Bayesian inference framework. By transposing core principles of logistics, such as supply chain management and inventory optimization, onto a probabilistic model, this research seeks to enhance decision-making processes in logistics. The significance of this study lies in its potential to revolutionize operational efficiency and adaptability in supply chains, contributing to both academic knowledge and practical applications in the logistics industry.

## Introduction

### Background of the Shifted Domain
The Shifted Domain represents a novel integration of logistics—a field focused on the efficient movement and storage of goods—with RxInfer.jl, a probabilistic programming tool designed for Bayesian inference. This fusion allows for enhanced modeling of complex systems, where uncertainty and interdependencies are prevalent.

### Significance and Novelty of the Research
This research is significant as it pioneers the application of probabilistic programming in logistics, offering a fresh perspective on traditional practices. The novelty lies in the development of probabilistic models that can adapt to real-time data, leading to improved operational strategies and customer satisfaction.

### Overarching Research Questions and Objectives
- How can logistics principles be effectively modeled using RxInfer.jl?
- What are the implications of applying probabilistic methods to traditional logistics practices?
- How does this integration enhance decision-making in supply chain management?

## Literature Review

### Historical Context of the Original Domains
- **Logistics**: Evolution from basic transportation to complex supply chain systems integrating technology.
