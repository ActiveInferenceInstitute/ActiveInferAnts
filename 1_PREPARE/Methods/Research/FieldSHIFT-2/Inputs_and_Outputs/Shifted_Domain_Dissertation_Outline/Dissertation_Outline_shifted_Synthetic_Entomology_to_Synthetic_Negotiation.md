# PhD Dissertation Plan: Transposing Entomology into Negotiation

## Executive Summary
This dissertation explores the innovative fusion of entomology and negotiation, proposing a groundbreaking framework that leverages biological principles to enhance negotiation strategies. By examining the adaptability, communication methods, and social structures inherent in insect behavior, this research aims to provide novel insights into negotiation dynamics. The potential impact includes improved negotiation outcomes across various contexts, including business, diplomacy, and conflict resolution. This interdisciplinary approach not only enriches the fields of negotiation and entomology but also fosters new paradigms for understanding complex human interactions.

## Introduction

### Background of the Shifted Domain
The integration of entomology, the study of insects, with negotiation, a dialogue aimed at reaching mutually beneficial agreements, presents a unique opportunity to draw parallels between biological behaviors and human interactions. Insects exhibit remarkable adaptability, communication strategies, and social structures that can inform and enhance negotiation practices.

### Significance and Novelty of the Research
This research is significant because it challenges traditional negotiation theories and introduces biological frameworks that emphasize adaptability and collaboration. The novelty lies in the application of entomological principles to negotiation, a domain that has not previously explored such interdisciplinary connections.

### Overarching Research Questions and Objectives
1. How can principles of metamorphosis in insects inform adaptive negotiation strategies?
2. In what ways can insect communication models enhance negotiation effectiveness?
3. How do social structures in insect colonies translate to team dynamics in negotiations?
