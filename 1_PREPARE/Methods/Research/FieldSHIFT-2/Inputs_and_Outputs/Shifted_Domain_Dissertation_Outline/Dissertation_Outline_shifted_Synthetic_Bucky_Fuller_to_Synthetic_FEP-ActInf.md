# PhD Dissertation Plan: Integrating Buckminster Fuller’s Design Science with the Free Energy Principle and Active Inference

## Executive Summary
This dissertation aims to explore the transformative potential of integrating Buckminster Fuller’s principles of design science with the Free Energy Principle (FEP) and Active Inference. By establishing a novel theoretical framework that merges sustainability with cognitive science, this research seeks to address complex global challenges through innovative design and adaptive cognitive systems. The implications of this integration extend across multiple disciplines, offering new insights into sustainability, cognition, and artificial intelligence. The dissertation will provide a comprehensive roadmap for future research, practical applications, and educational innovations.

## Introduction
### Background of the Shifted Domain
The Shifted Domain represents a fusion of two distinct fields: Buckminster Fuller’s design science, which emphasizes sustainability and efficiency, and the Free Energy Principle, which focuses on minimizing variational free energy in adaptive systems. This integration allows for a holistic understanding of both ecological and cognitive processes.

### Significance and Novelty of the Research
This research is significant because it bridges the gap between ecological sustainability and cognitive science, creating a new interdisciplinary framework. The novelty lies in applying Fuller's design principles to cognitive architectures, leading to sustainable and adaptive systems that can address pressing global issues.

### Overarching Research Questions and Objectives
- How can Buckminster Fuller’s principles inform the development of cognitive architectures based on the Free Energy Principle?
- In what ways can sustainability be integrated into cognitive models to enhance adaptability and efficiency?
- What are the implications of this integration for both cognitive science and design practices?
