# Executive Summary

This dissertation explores the transformative potential of integrating health care with the Spatial Web, an innovative fusion of physical and digital environments. By examining the intersections of patient-centered care and user-centric design, this research aims to develop novel methodologies that enhance patient experience, improve health outcomes, and ensure secure data management. The significance of this work lies in its ability to redefine health care delivery in the digital age, offering a roadmap for future research, practical applications, and interdisciplinary collaborations that can yield substantial societal impacts.

# Introduction

## Background of the Shifted Domain
The Shifted Domain merges health care, a field focused on the well-being of individuals, with the Spatial Web, which integrates physical and digital spaces. This fusion presents opportunities to leverage advanced technologies such as AR, VR, and blockchain to enhance patient care and operational efficiency.

## Significance and Novelty of the Research
This research is significant as it addresses the gaps in traditional health care delivery by incorporating immersive technologies and decentralized data management systems. The novelty lies in proposing new frameworks for patient education, monitoring, and interaction that are rooted in the principles of both domains.

## Overarching Research Questions and Objectives
1. How can AR and VR technologies improve patient education and engagement in health care?
2. What role do digital twins play in chronic disease management?
3. How can blockchain technology enhance the security and integrity of health data sharing?
