# PhD Dissertation Plan: A Shifted Domain from Chemistry to Astronomy

## Executive Summary
This dissertation explores the innovative intersection of chemistry and astronomy, termed "astrochemistry," which investigates the chemical processes occurring in celestial environments. The significance of this research lies in its potential to reshape our understanding of stellar nucleosynthesis, the formation of celestial bodies, and the dynamics of the universe. By integrating chemical principles into astronomical studies, this research aims to provide a comprehensive framework for understanding cosmic phenomena, ultimately leading to advancements in both fields and contributing to global challenges such as sustainability and resource management.

## Introduction

### Background of the Shifted Domain
The Shifted Domain represents a transformative integration of chemistry and astronomy, leveraging the foundational principles of chemical reactions, molecular structures, and thermodynamics to elucidate processes occurring in the cosmos. This interdisciplinary approach opens new avenues for research and innovation, allowing for a deeper understanding of the universe's evolution.

### Significance and Novelty of the Research
The novelty of this research lies in its ability to apply chemical methodologies to astronomical phenomena, thereby creating a new sub-discipline. This integration holds the potential for groundbreaking discoveries in the formation of elements, the dynamics of galaxies, and the interactions between dark matter and baryonic matter.

### Overarching Research Questions and Objectives
1. How can chemical principles be applied to understand stellar nucleosynthesis and cosmic events?
2. What new theoretical constructs emerge from the integration of chemistry and astronomy?
3. How does this Shifted Domain inform our understanding of the universe's evolution and the formation of celestial bodies?
