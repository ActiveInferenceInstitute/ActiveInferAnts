# PhD Dissertation Plan: Transformative Insights from Guest Experience in Hospitality to Mitochondrial Function in Cellular Biology

## Executive Summary
This dissertation aims to explore the innovative intersection between guest experience in the hospitality industry and mitochondrial function in cellular biology. By leveraging principles from hospitality, such as personalization, holistic interactions, and service quality, this research seeks to develop a framework for personalized mitochondrial therapies. The potential impact includes advancing mitochondrial medicine, enhancing cellular health, and addressing critical health challenges. This work will pave the way for interdisciplinary collaborations and novel educational approaches, ultimately transforming both fields.

## Introduction

### Background of the Shifted Domain
The hospitality industry has long prioritized guest experience, focusing on creating personalized and meaningful interactions. Concurrently, mitochondrial biology has emerged as a crucial field for understanding cellular health and disease. This dissertation proposes a novel framework that integrates principles from guest experience with mitochondrial function, creating a Shifted Domain that offers fresh insights into both fields.

### Significance and Novelty of the Research
This research is significant as it challenges traditional disciplinary boundaries, proposing that insights from hospitality can inform and enhance mitochondrial biology. By adopting a holistic and personalized approach, this dissertation aims to contribute to the development of innovative therapies and improve our understanding of cellular processes.

### Overarching Research Questions and Objectives
- How can principles of guest experience be transposed to enhance our understanding of mitochondrial function?
- What are the potential implications of personalized mitochondrial therapies on cellular health?
- How can this interdisciplinary approach inform future research and practical applications in both fields?
