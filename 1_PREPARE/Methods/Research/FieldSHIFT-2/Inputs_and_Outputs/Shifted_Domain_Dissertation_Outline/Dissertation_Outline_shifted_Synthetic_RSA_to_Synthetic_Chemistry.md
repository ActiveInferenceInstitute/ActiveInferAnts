# PhD Dissertation Plan: Transformative Domain Shift from RSA Cryptography to Chemistry

## Executive Summary
This dissertation explores the innovative integration of RSA cryptography principles into the field of chemistry, creating a novel interdisciplinary domain that enhances security, verification, and optimization of chemical processes. By establishing parallels between cryptographic mechanisms and chemical reactions, this research aims to develop new theoretical frameworks, methodologies, and practical applications that can significantly impact both fields. The potential outcomes include improved safety protocols in chemical synthesis, the development of secure chemical databases, and a paradigm shift in how chemical reactions are understood and managed.

## Introduction
### Background of the Shifted Domain
The fusion of RSA cryptography, which relies on mathematical principles for secure communication, with chemistry, the science of matter and its transformations, presents an unprecedented opportunity to innovate. This shift not only allows for a deeper understanding of chemical processes but also introduces a new lens through which to examine security and verification in chemical practices.

### Significance and Novelty of the Research
This research is significant as it challenges traditional boundaries between disciplines, offering a fresh perspective on chemical reactions as secure processes. The novelty lies in applying concepts such as encryption, key management, and digital signatures to chemical synthesis, thereby enhancing the integrity and reliability of chemical practices.

### Overarching Research Questions and Objectives
1. How can principles of RSA cryptography be applied to optimize chemical reactions?
2. What new theoretical constructs emerge from the integration of cryptographic and chemical principles?
3. In what ways can the concept of molecular keys transform current practices in chemical synthesis and verification?
