# PhD Dissertation Plan: Transforming Cuisine through the Lens of Guest Experience

## Executive Summary
This dissertation aims to explore the innovative intersection of guest experience and cuisine, proposing a transformative framework that enhances culinary practices through principles derived from hospitality management. By integrating personalized guest interactions, service quality, and sustainability into culinary experiences, this research seeks to redefine how food is prepared, presented, and consumed. The potential impact of this research spans multiple domains, including hospitality, culinary arts, and sustainability, paving the way for new methodologies and practices that prioritize guest engagement and satisfaction.

## Introduction

### Background of the Shifted Domain
The Shifted Domain of "Transforming Cuisine through the Lens of Guest Experience" represents an innovative fusion of hospitality management and culinary arts. This interdisciplinary approach recognizes that the dining experience is not solely about the food but encompasses all interactions and perceptions that guests encounter throughout their culinary journey.

### Significance and Novelty of the Research
This research is significant as it challenges traditional culinary practices by emphasizing the importance of guest experience in shaping culinary outcomes. By applying guest experience principles to cuisine, the study introduces novel concepts such as Culinary Experience Management (CEM) and Taste Blueprinting, fostering a new understanding of how to create memorable dining experiences.

### Overarching Research Questions and Objectives
- How can principles of guest experience enhance culinary practices?
- What are the implications of personalized dining experiences on customer satisfaction and loyalty?
- How can technology be leveraged to improve the culinary experience?
- What role does sustainability play in shaping modern culinary practices?
